Section 01

The Harvics × MAFI Product Routing Map

Each product category is treated as a completely separate commercial vertical. Every line is mapped to a precise market, buyer class, route-to-market model, and price/margin architecture before a single introduction is activated.

🍅 Tomato Paste & Concentrate Primary Route

Germany leads European tomato paste imports at 18% share — the single largest market. Egypt is not China. European buyers are actively diversifying away from Chinese tomato paste, and Egyptian origin — Mediterranean, clean, LEED Gold certified — currently commands a premium in Germany and the UK.

Germany — 18% share
Industrial food manufacturers, sauce producers, EU redistribution hubs. Format: bulk aseptic drums. Annual volume contracts and supply agreements.
United Kingdom — 14% share
Bestway Cash & Carry (Harvics direct connection) — 65,000+ ethnic food retail points. Secondary: UK food manufacturers producing ready meals, ketchup, pasta sauces.
Italy / Spain — 14% combined
Primary processors importing Egyptian concentrate to blend and re-export as premium Italian-origin product. Egyptian origin commands premium as EU domestic farming shrinks.
Vietnam — Overflow Route
Growing processed-food industry. Lower price point but extremely high volume. Ideal for clearing production that does not meet EU premium specifications.
🍊 Citrus Concentrates — Orange, Lemon, Grapefruit Crown Jewel

Crown-jewel category — highest strategic priority. Eckes-Granini is already active as the primary anchor buyer. They cannot absorb 100% of capacity.

Eckes-Granini — Primary
Already active. Granini, Pago, Joker brands. Raw material buyer. No conflict with overflow routes.
United Kingdom — Overflow
Bestway (Harvics direct connection) — private-label juice programs. Complementary, not conflicting with Eckes-Granini.
Scandinavia
Sweden & Norway pipelines already under active outreach. Private-label manufacturers who are not Eckes-Granini clients.
GCC + North America
UAE/Saudi: premium halal-certified citrus juice. USA: Bajco Group (Harvics direct connection), Northeast corridor manufacturers.
Harvics routes the identical product to private-label manufacturers in the UK and Scandinavia who are not Eckes-Granini clients. Complementary, never conflicting.

Section 02

The Harvics Dedicated Desk Model

30% Harvics proprietary commercial infrastructure. Not a trading-house model. A dedicated commercial engine that operates like MAFI's own European sales office — without MAFI having to build it, staff it, or manage it.

01
Full-Time Dedication
One senior commercial desk lead, 100% allocated to MAFI. One supporting analyst/coordinator. Zero shared portfolio responsibility.
02
Dedicated Infrastructure
Custom CRM, dedicated email alias, communication channels, shared drive with routing maps, buyer dossiers, and compliance folders.
03
Buyer Database
200+ pre-qualified accounts across Germany, UK, Scandinavia, GCC, USA, and Asia-Pacific — segmented by category and buyer class.
04
Weekly Dashboard
Every Monday morning: pipeline value, conversion probability, next actions, blockers. One contact, full accountability.
05
Scalable Structure
Phase II: lead + coordinator. Phase III: add transaction coordinator. Phase IV: full mini-team of 3–4 people, still 100% dedicated.
06
Performance-Gated
After 3-month minimum, if 70% of priority target list hasn't been engaged, MAFI may exit without further obligation beyond fees already paid.
When Harvics onboards MAFI, we don't add your products to a shared trading desk. We build you a dedicated commercial sales office inside Harvics. One senior person works on MAFI and only MAFI, full time. They live inside the routing map we created together. You get all the benefit of a professional European sales team without hiring anyone, without paying salaries, without managing infrastructure. This dedicated desk is what the retainer actually buys — not vague promises, but owned commercial capacity. That is the Harvics difference.

Funding: The entire desk is funded through the Phase II Retained Market Activation retainer (USD 20,000/month, 3-month minimum). Not a commission — the infrastructure fee that delivers a zero-overhead European export sales team working only on MAFI's portfolio.

Network Protection: All buyer data, routing logic, and commercial architecture remain Harvics proprietary assets. MAFI receives outputs and results — never raw network files unless exclusivity is granted in writing.

Section 04

Commercial Model

Phased, retained, and performance-linked. Qualification to activation, activation to execution, execution to scale — only where commercial discipline and results justify expansion.

Phase I Commercial Admission & Strategic Qualification USD 25,000

One-time admission fee. Payable in advance. Non-refundable upon activation.

  • Mandate intake and category, territory & buyer-class review
  • Product-to-market fit analysis and routing architecture
  • Commercial framework design and activation-readiness review
  • Exclusivity feasibility assessment
  • Output: qualification note, scope matrix, routing framework, engagement-path recommendation
Phase II Retained Market Activation USD 20,000/mo

Monthly retainer. Minimum 3-month commitment. Payable monthly in advance. Activates the Dedicated Desk.

  • Target account mapping and buyer prioritization
  • Controlled introductions and meeting coordination
  • Commercial follow-up and pipeline development
  • Sample & compliance coordination
  • Output: active pipeline, qualified buyer discussions, weekly dashboard
Phase III Execution & Transaction Conversion USD 25,000+/mo

Applies once commercial activity matures into executable business. Economics defined by category complexity, geography, and transaction size.

  • Transaction structuring and order-path support
  • Principal-buyer or distribution route handling
  • Commercial basis: execution retainer / transaction-linked fee / principal-buying spread / hybrid
  • Output: first executable transaction route, revenue conversion framework
Phase IV Scale, Rights & Exclusivity Negotiated

Applies only after performance, alignment, and commercial validity are established. Not automatic. Subject to written confirmation only.

  • Territorial and category expansion
  • Exclusivity discussion and performance thresholds
  • Revised commercial economics where scale justifies them
  • Output: expansion recommendation, exclusivity assessment, revised rights structure

Section 09

Our Approach

Eight principles that define how Harvics operates — and what distinguishes this engagement from any conventional agent, broker, or trading-house arrangement.

01
Selective, Not General
Each supply-side partner is assessed selectively, structured selectively, and activated only within agreed scope. No open-book engagement.
02
Product-Specific, Not Broad
Each MAFI product line is treated separately. No category is pushed through the same route or buyer class as another.
03
Market-Routed, Not Random
Each category is assigned to the market, buyer type, and commercial path most likely to produce qualified revenue — before activation.
04
Controlled Activation, Not Open Circulation
Buyer access is opened in a controlled sequence. Market access is never released casually or without structure.
05
Dedicated Handling, Not Shared Attention
A supplier of MAFI's scale is not handled as one of many undifferentiated accounts. Dedicated capacity ensures continuity and accountability.
06
Commercial Before Transactional
The first priority is to architect the route properly — category, market, buyer class, and commercial structure. Transaction comes after route discipline.
07
Retained Discipline, Not Speculative
Market architecture, buyer activation, and execution capacity are deployed only under formal engagement — never on open-ended or speculative terms.
08
Scale Only on Performance
Territorial rights, broader activation, and exclusivity are assessed and expanded only where performance, alignment, and commercial discipline justify them.
