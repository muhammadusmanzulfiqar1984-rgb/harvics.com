## COMPETITOR ANALYSIS: GLOBAL TRADING HOUSES

### **Glencore** (World's largest commodity trader)
- Logo: Simple blue text + "G" mark
- Strategy: Conservative, trusted, institutional
- Feel: Power through simplicity

### **Cargill** (Agricultural commodities)
- Logo: Green leaf + text
- Strategy: Natural, heritage, American
- Feel: Agricultural roots, sustainability

### **Vitol** (Oil trading giant)
- Logo: Red arrow + text
- Strategy: Movement, flow, energy
- Feel: Dynamic, directional

### **Trafigura** (Commodities + logistics)
- Logo: Orange/red, bold text
- Strategy: Energy, confidence
- Feel: Modern trading house

### **Japanese Sogo Shosha (General Trading Companies):**

**Mitsubishi Corporation:**
- Logo: Three red diamonds
- Meaning: Merger of 3 companies (三菱 = three water chestnuts)
- Strategy: Heritage, Japanese precision, diversification

**Mitsui & Co:**
- Logo: Circle mark + text
- Meaning: Unity, global reach
- Strategy: Traditional meets modern

**Marubeni:**
- Logo: Red circle (maru = circle in Japanese)
- Meaning: Perfection, completeness, global
- Strategy: Simple, memorable, Asian heritage

---

## KEY INSIGHT FOR HARVICS:

**Trading houses use SIMPLE, POWERFUL symbols:**
- Arrows = Movement/flow
- Circles = Global/complete
- Diamonds = Value/precision
- Geometric = Trust/stability

**NOT complex tech logos. They show COMMERCE not CODE.**

---

## WHAT HARVICS LOGO MUST COMMUNICATE:

1. **GLOBAL TRADE FLOWS** - Goods/money moving worldwide
2. **MULTI-COMMODITY** - 10 industries (not just one like Cargill)
3. **FINANCIAL POWER** - "We sell money" (HPAY)
4. **INTELLIGENCE** - Tech advantage over old traders
5. **TRUST** - Handle billions in transactions
6. **EMERGING MARKETS** - Not Western-centric

---

## DESIGN DIRECTION:

### **Concept: "The Global Exchange"**

**Visual Elements:**
- **Arrows/Flows**: Representing trade, sourcing, movement
- **Circle/Hub**: Central exchange point
- **Gold**: Money, value, commodities
- **Maroon**: Heritage, trust, power

**Symbolic Meaning:**
- Multiple flows converging = Sourcing from everywhere
- Central hub = HARVICS as the exchange
- Bidirectional = Buy AND sell
- Global circle = Worldwide reach
